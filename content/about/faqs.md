    questions: 
      - question: What is the difference between a manufactured home and a modular home?
        answer: >-
          The biggest difference is the **building code**. 


          * **Modular homes** are built to **local/state building codes**—the same as site-built homes. They are placed on permanent foundations and can appreciate in value. 

          * **Manufactured homes** are built to the federal **HUD Code**, on a permanent steel chassis, and may face zoning or financing limitations. 


          Modular homes generally offer more customization, higher long-term value and more flexibility in placement. 

      - question: How is a factory-built home different from a site-built home?
        answer: >-
          The difference is where construction happens. Factory-built homes are constructed **indoors**, protecting materials from weather and ensuring consistent quality. Site-built homes are built outdoors and can face delays or material exposure.
